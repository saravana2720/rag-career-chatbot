# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

## 1. What career should I choose as a fresher?
As a fresher, choose a career based on your interest and strengths. Common beginner-friendly roles are Software Engineer, Data Analyst, and QA Engineer. Focus on fundamentals and projects first.

---

## 2. Is coding mandatory for IT jobs?
Coding is mandatory for most technical roles like Software Engineer, Data Scientist, and Backend Developer. However, non-coding roles like Business Analyst, Product Manager, and Tech Support also exist.

---

## 3. Which programming language should I learn first?
Python is highly recommended for beginners because it is easy to learn and widely used in software development, data science, and AI.

---

## 4. How many projects are required to get a job?
Quality matters more than quantity.  
- Freshers: 2–4 strong projects  
- Experienced: Real-world or production-level projects  

---

## 5. What is ATS and why is it important?
ATS (Applicant Tracking System) filters resumes using keywords before recruiters see them. An ATS-friendly resume increases your chance of getting interview calls.

---

## 6. How long does it take to become job-ready?
It depends on consistency and learning speed:
- Basics + projects: 3–6 months  
- Advanced roles (ML, DevOps): 6–12 months  

---

## 7. Are certifications mandatory for jobs?
Certifications are not mandatory but they help validate skills, especially for freshers and cloud roles like AWS or Azure.

---

## 8. What is the difference between Frontend and Backend?
- Frontend: UI, UX, client-side (HTML, CSS, React)
- Backend: Server-side logic, databases, APIs (Python, Java, Node.js)

---

## 9. How important are DSA and problem-solving?
DSA is very important for product-based companies and interviews. It improves logical thinking and coding confidence.

---

## 10. Can I switch careers into IT from non-IT background?
Yes. Many people switch from non-IT backgrounds. Focus on fundamentals, projects, and consistent practice.

---

## 11. What skills do recruiters look for besides coding?
Recruiters also value:
- Communication
- Problem-solving
- Teamwork
- Learning attitude

---

## 12. How should I prepare for interviews?
- Revise fundamentals
- Practice DSA
- Prepare project explanations
- Practice behavioral questions (STAR method)

---

## 13. Is AI going to replace software jobs?
AI will change jobs, not replace all of them. Engineers who learn AI tools and adapt will have more opportunities.

---

## 14. Should I focus on CGPA or skills?
Skills and projects matter more than CGPA. A decent CGPA is enough if you have strong practical skills.
