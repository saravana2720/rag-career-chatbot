# Resume Tips for Experienced Professionals

## Professional Summary
- Keep it concise (3–4 lines)
- Highlight your role, experience, and core expertise
- Mention key achievements, not responsibilities

**Example:**
Results-driven Backend Developer with 5+ years of experience building scalable APIs and microservices using Python and AWS.

---

## Work Experience (Most Important Section)
- Use reverse chronological order
- Focus on **impact**, not tasks
- Use numbers and metrics where possible

**Good Example:**
- Designed and deployed REST APIs handling 1M+ requests/day
- Improved system performance by 30% through optimization

---

## Skills Section
- Prioritize role-relevant skills
- Group skills by category

**Example:**
- Languages: Python, Java  
- Frameworks: Flask, Spring Boot  
- Databases: MySQL, MongoDB  
- Cloud/DevOps: AWS, Docker, CI/CD  

---

## Projects
- Mention only **relevant** projects
- Production or client projects preferred
- Clearly explain your contribution

**Format:**
- Project Name (Company / Client)
- Problem & Solution
- Tech Stack
- Impact

---

## Certifications
- Add certifications that support your experience
- Mention year of completion

**Example:**
AWS Certified Solutions Architect – Associate (2024)

---

## Education
- Keep it short
- Degree, college, year
- CGPA optional for experienced professionals

---

## ATS Optimization Tips
- Match keywords from job description
- Use standard headings
- Avoid tables, images, and icons
- Save resume as PDF

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Listing every technology you know
- Writing long paragraphs
- Repeating job descriptions
- Using outdated or irrelevant skills

---

## Resume Length
- 1 page (up to 5 years experience)
- 2 pages (6+ years experience)

---

## Final Checklist
- ✔ Impact-focused bullet points  
- ✔ Updated skills  
- ✔ ATS-friendly format  
- ✔ Error-free language  
- ✔ Job-specific customization
